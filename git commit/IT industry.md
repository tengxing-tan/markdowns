# 1. Functional Sub-Sectors

The software market is typically divided into four primary functional groups: 

- **Enterprise Software**: The largest segment in Malaysia (estimated **US$1.16 billion in 2025**). It includes:
    - **ERP** (Enterprise Resource Planning): Managing core business processes like finance and supply chain.
    - **CRM** (Customer Relationship Management): Tools like Salesforce or HubSpot for managing client interactions.
    - **BI & Analytics** (Business Intelligence): Software for data-driven decision-making, such as Tableau or IBM tools.
- **Productivity Software**: Applications that support daily tasks like producing documents, spreadsheets, and presentations (e.g., Microsoft Office, Google Workspace, and Zoom).
- **System Infrastructure Software**: The framework for managing hardware and organizational processes, including **Cybersecurity** (McAfee, Cisco) and storage management.
- **Application Development Software**: Tools and platforms used to create other software, such as **Amazon Web Services (AWS)** and **Visual Studio**. 

# 2. Vertical (Industry-Specific) Software

Many software companies specialize in "Vertical Applications" tailored to specific industries: 

- **Fintech**: Financial technology for digital banking, payments, and crypto.
- **Healthtech**: Electronic medical records (EMR) and AI diagnostics for healthcare.
- **EdTech**: Educational technology for online learning and training systems.
- **AgriTech**: Precision farming tools, sensors, and automation for agriculture.
- **Industrial Software**: Specialized tools for manufacturing, such as **CAD** (Computer-Aided Design) and **SCM** (Supply Chain Management). 

# 3. Delivery & Business Models

Software is also categorized by how it is provided to the customer: 

- **SaaS (Software as a Service)**: Subscription-based cloud software. The Malaysian SaaS market is estimated to reach **USD 6.28 billion by 2030**.
- **PaaS (Platform as a Service)**: Providing a platform for customers to develop, run, and manage applications.
- **IaaS (Infrastructure as a Service)**: Providing fundamental computing resources like virtual machines (e.g., Azure, AWS).
- **Open Source**: Software provided for free with paid support or customization services. 

# 4. Creative & Digital Content (Emerging in Malaysia)

Malaysia has a rapidly growing sub-sector focused on digital creativity: 

- **Gaming & eSports**: Development of interactive games and competitive platforms.
- **Animation & VFX**: Creative digital content for movies and media.
